HISTORY

The Secret Village is a brand name of the BIZ Reguliersdwarsstraat, where BIZ stands for Business Investment Zone. This is a new legal structure where all businesses on the street are compulsory members and therefore all contribute to the activities of the BIZ. This is to prevent individuals from benefiting from improvements without sharing the costs.

The BIZ Reguliersdwarsstraat was established on January 1, 2015, evolving from the Business Association Reguliersdwarsstraat founded in July 2011. With greater financial resources, the BIZ aimed to attract more visitors to the street, through initiatives like purchasing festive lighting, organizing events, and adding greenery and flower boxes to the street.

For marketing and promotion, a completely new name was chosen as the area covered by the BIZ does not entirely align with the Reguliersdwarsstraat itself. It exclusively includes the eastern part of the street, as well as the side streets Geelvinckssteeg, Openhartsteeg, and Sint Jorisstraat.

The concept of Secret Village was developed by Tanja van der Knoop, who arrived in Reguliersdwarsstraat in October 2015 with ideas to make the street green, sustainable, and attractive again. This appealed to the BIZ board, and she became the leader of an external project team, described as ambitious and determined.

The original working title "Green Village" was replaced with the more intriguing "Secret Village". "Secret" refers to the hidden 18th-century canal gardens where various restaurants have beautiful terraces. The term "Village" not only evokes Gay Village but also the intimate, almost village-like character of the central part of the street.
